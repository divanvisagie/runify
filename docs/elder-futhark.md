# Elder Futhark

The Elder Futhark is the oldest form of the runic alphabets, used by Germanic peoples from the 2nd to 8th/9th century CE. It consists of 24 runes arranged in three ættir (groups of 8).

## Historical Context

- **Period**: 2nd to 8th/9th century CE
- **Rune count**: 24 runes
- **Structure**: Three ættir (Freyr's, Hagal's, Tyr's)
- **Evidence**: The Vadstena bracteate (6th century) confirms the ætt sequence

The Elder Futhark was later simplified to 16 runes (Younger Futhark) around 800 CE.

## The 24 Runes

### First Ætt (Freyr's Ætt)

| Rune | Latin | Name       | Proto-Germanic Meaning |
|------|-------|------------|------------------------|
| ᚠ    | f     | *fehu      | cattle, wealth         |
| ᚢ    | u     | *ūruz      | aurochs                |
| ᚦ    | þ (th)| *þurisaz   | giant, thorn           |
| ᚨ    | a     | *ansuz     | god (Odin)             |
| ᚱ    | r     | *raidō     | ride, journey          |
| ᚲ    | k     | *kaunan    | torch, ulcer           |
| ᚷ    | g     | *gebō      | gift                   |
| ᚹ    | w     | *wunjō     | joy                    |

### Second Ætt (Hagal's Ætt)

| Rune | Latin | Name       | Proto-Germanic Meaning       |
|------|-------|------------|------------------------------|
| ᚺ    | h     | *haglaz    | hail                         |
| ᚾ    | n     | *nauðiz    | need, distress               |
| ᛁ    | i     | *īsaz      | ice                          |
| ᛃ    | j     | *jēra      | year, harvest                |
| ᛇ    | ï/ei  | *eihwaz    | yew tree                     |
| ᛈ    | p     | *perþō     | unknown (possibly pear tree) |
| ᛉ    | z     | *algiz     | elk, protection              |
| ᛊ/ᛋ  | s     | *sōwilō    | sun                          |

### Third Ætt (Tyr's Ætt)

| Rune | Latin   | Name       | Proto-Germanic Meaning |
|------|---------|------------|------------------------|
| ᛏ    | t       | *tīwaz     | Tyr (god)              |
| ᛒ    | b       | *berkanan  | birch                  |
| ᛖ    | e       | *ehwaz     | horse                  |
| ᛗ    | m       | *mannaz    | man, human             |
| ᛚ    | l       | *laguz     | water, lake            |
| ᛜ    | ŋ (ng)  | *ingwaz    | Ing (god/hero)         |
| ᛞ    | d       | *dagaz     | day                    |
| ᛟ    | o       | *ōþala     | heritage, estate       |

### Phonetic Mappings

When phonetic mode is enabled (default), digraphs are converted to single runes:

| Input | Rune | Notes                    |
|-------|------|--------------------------|
| th    | ᚦ    | Thorn sound              |
| ng    | ᛜ    | Velar nasal              |
| ing   | ᛜ    | Common suffix            |
| nk    | ᛜᚲ   | Composite                |
| ch    | ᚷ    | Germanic /x/ or /ɣ/      |
| x     | ᚲᛋ   | /ks/ sound               |

### Non-Historical Mappings

## Unicode Reference

Elder Futhark runes occupy Unicode range U+16A0 to U+16EA (Runic block):

```
ᚠ U+16A0  ᚢ U+16A2  ᚦ U+16A6  ᚨ U+16A8  ᚱ U+16B1  ᚲ U+16B2
ᚷ U+16B7  ᚹ U+16B9  ᚺ U+16BA  ᚾ U+16BE  ᛁ U+16C1  ᛃ U+16C3
ᛇ U+16C7  ᛈ U+16C8  ᛉ U+16C9  ᛊ U+16CA  ᛋ U+16CB  ᛏ U+16CF
ᛒ U+16D2  ᛖ U+16D6  ᛗ U+16D7  ᛚ U+16DA  ᛜ U+16DC  ᛞ U+16DE
ᛟ U+16DF
```

Note: ᛊ (U+16CA) and ᛋ (U+16CB) are variant forms of sowilo.
